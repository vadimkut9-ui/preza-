🎯 Main Goal 2 / 6

Goal of the Elective Course

To transition from reproductive problem-solving to creative application of number theory concepts.

🎓

Key Focus

Teach students to use the Euclidean algorithm, properties of primes, modular arithmetic, and Diophantine equations to solve non‑standard problems, including OGE Task No. 19.

📈

Expected Outcome

Develop deep understanding of divisibility, congruences, and integer equations, enabling confident handling of advanced exam and olympiad tasks.

📋 Research Tasks (from dissertation) 3 / 6

Key Objectives of the Study

  • Summarize the significance of number theory in the school mathematics curriculum
  • Examine the basic concepts of number theory studied in the school mathematics course
  • Analyze educational and methodological literature on the research topic
  • Develop a program for the elective course "Elements of Number Theory" for 10th grade students
  • Conduct a pedagogical experiment and analyze its results
  • Develop technological maps for the elective course lessons on "Divisibility Rules" and "Solving OGE Task No. 19"

📌 Ultimate goal: Bridge the gap between elementary arithmetic (grades 5–6) and high-level exam problems (OGE Task 19, olympiads).

🧑‍🏫 Teaching & Research Methods 4 / 6

Methods and Organization

🔹 Pedagogical Principles

  • From simple to complex (gradual difficulty increase)
  • Systematic and sequential delivery
  • Step‑by‑step mastery of solution logic
  • Connection to OGE and olympiad problems

🔹 Forms of Work

  • Lectures with interactive discussions
  • Problem‑solving workshops and practice sessions
  • Group research and mini‑projects (Sieve of Eratosthenes, friendly numbers)
  • Independent work and diagnostic tests
  • Detailed analysis of OGE Task No. 19 solutions

📊 Research methods used: theoretical analysis, curriculum review, pedagogical observation, experimental comparison (control vs. experimental groups), and statistical evaluation.
